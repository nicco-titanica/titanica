# 05 — UX

Status: **PROPOSED (v0.1)** — governing document, awaiting founder ratification
Derives from: `01-PRODUCT.md` (§3, §4, §5, §6), `03-DATABASE.md` (§3, §4, §5, §6, §8),
`00-CONSTITUTION.md`
Unlocks: the `apps/web` experience build (paired with `04-DESIGN.md` for the visual
language)

> This document owns **behavior, flow, and felt experience.** It does **not** own the
> visual language — typography, color, the arrow, spacing — which is `04-DESIGN`'s
> territory. Where the two meet ("the threshold should feel like ceremony"), `05` states
> the *intent* and `04` states the *means*. Every experience below is anchored by name to
> a fact in `03-DATABASE`, so nothing here is invented.
> The interface is not where attention is captured; it is where standing is felt. Every
> screen answers to the Catalog's permanence and the member's belonging — never to a
> metric.

---

## 1. The one principle

**Institution, not a feed. Reward depth, not dwell time.** The Constitution's ladder,
applied to interface. The modern web's defaults — infinite scroll, streaks, notification
bait, dwell-time optimization — are engineered to extract attention; they are therefore
forbidden here (§9). *Beauty increases attention* (Constitution): we earn attention by
being worth reading, never by trapping. If a design choice would raise a usage metric
while weakening the House as a permanent institution, the Decision Rule kills it.

---

## 2. Two surfaces over one Catalog

Derived from `03 §4` (two depths) and `01 §3–§4`.

**The public surface — the vitrine.** Renders `thesis` only. Draws from the curated
rotating window (`featuredInWindow` / `windowOrder`, `03 §4.1`) — a taste-proving few,
not a paywalled feed. In Phase 0 the door is deliberately opaque (`01 §5`); *how* opaque
is an open question (§10).

**The member surface — depth, totality, the mind.** Renders `thesis + apparatus`. Reaches
every Work where `now ≥ memberReleaseAt`, archive included — the totality invariant
(`03 §4.1`): nothing rotates out of a member's reach.

Same Work, two states. The step from `thesis` to `apparatus` is a **designed reveal** —
the member crossing into the full argument — not a hidden section that merely happens to
load.

---

## 3. The threshold — entry is induction, not signup

The single most important screen in the House. `01 §3c`: entry is induction into an
institution, not a signup.

**Entry is always validated — never self-serve** (founder-ratified). No door lets someone
admit themselves. Invitees are *pre-validated* (already chosen by the House); everyone else
passes through an application the House approves. There is no path from "arrive" to "inside"
that the House has not personally cleared.

**What it must be.** The person is *named*. A crossing is *marked*. The `admittedAt` moment
is commemorated, not stored as a silent timestamp. The FOUNDING designation (`03 §5`,
`cohort`) is **tracked in the back, not surfaced in the front** (founder-ratified) — the
House knows who its first fifty are; the interface does not wear it as a badge. It is a
forum-style distinction to be used later, if ever, not a launch feature.

**What it must never be.** "Create your free account." A form. A transaction. A progress
bar nudging you to "complete your profile."

The three doors (`03 §5`, `admissionPath`) share one register — *you were chosen* — and
all resolve to a House-validated admission:

- **DIRECT_INVITE** — the named invitation. The person arrives already validated; the
  threshold confirms it.
- **NOMINATION** — arrival via a member's scarce nomination (§8), then House validation.
  The threshold acknowledges the sponsor without letting entry feel transactional.
- **APPLICATION** — an application the House validates (the public door in Phase 3, but the
  validation mechanism exists from Stage 3). The threshold is the *acceptance*, and it must
  read as selection, never as purchase.

---

## 4. Precedence as ritual

`01 §4` / `03 §4`: members see a Work before the public. The gap between `memberReleaseAt`
and `publicReleaseAt` is data; the **ceremony is UX's to build.**

The member's prior sight is marked — *this is yours first, before the world.* Not a silent
`earlyAccess` flag; a moment that makes belonging felt. Whatever the gap's length, the
member experiences precedence as a **conferred privilege**, not a technical head start.

**Dormant at launch.** In v1, Dispatches are public and Dossiers are member-only forever
(`NEVER`), so no Work is released member-*first-then-public* — precedence is designed and
ready but not yet exercised (`03 §11.1`). The ritual is built when the first member-first
release actually occurs.

---

## 5. Reading a Work — depth, not dwell

The public reads the `thesis`: the promise, the proof of taste. The member reads the
`apparatus`: the reward — the full argument, the sources, the scoped claims, the
reasoning.

No mechanic that optimizes for time-on-page: no infinite scroll, no autoplaying "next,"
no engineered rabbit-hole of "related" Works, no streaks or badges. Reading time
(`03` metadata) is shown as *respect for the reader's hours* — honest information, never a
gamified meter.

The Work is presented as an object with permanence: its **frozen code** (`03 §3.2`) is
visible as archival identity — a citation, a fact that will not move. The reader should
feel they are reading something that will still be here, unchanged, in ten years.

## 6. Navigating the Catalog

Two axes, matching `03`'s two structures:

- **The permanent taxonomy** — Kingdom → Domain (`03 §3.1`). The architecture of the
  House: how the Catalog is *filed*. Stable and legible.
- **Lens** — cross-dimensional (`03 §3.3`). How the Catalog is *thought through*:
  discovery, not filing.

Public navigation is curated and thin — the rotating window (§2). Member navigation is
**total** — every Work, the full archive, nothing rotated beyond reach. Member search sees
the member scope; the public scope leaks nothing (`03 §8` / `§7.7`). The membrane holds
even inside the search box.

---

## 7. Day one — what is real, what is deliberately absent

`01 §3`. On the first day the member finds **three real things**:

- the **Catalog in the member relationship** — depth, totality, the precedence ritual
  (§2, §4);
- the **mind behind the House** — held for now inside `apparatus` (`03 §9.1` stub; not
  resolved here);
- the **frame of having been chosen** — carried forward from the threshold (§3).

And **two things are deliberately absent**, because faking them kills the patent faster
than their absence:

- the **peer circle** — a promise seeded toward (`01 §3`, Phase 1–2), *not* a day-one
  room. UX must not stand up an empty community space, a ghost-town feed, or a members
  directory that betrays how few are inside.
- **bespoke intelligence** — deferred (`01 §3`). Not a launch surface.

The discipline of day one is **restraint**: show what is real; hide nothing whose
emptiness would reveal thinness.

---

## 8. Nomination — the member's scarce right

`03 §6`, `01 §6`. The member holds a scarce right of nomination (`03`,
`nominationAllowance`).

The experience makes scarcity *felt*: the remaining allowance is visible and finite
("two nominations this year"), so a nomination is spent, not sprayed. The member
**nominates** — makes a case (`03`, `candidateContext`) — and does **not** admit. The UX
must never imply the member grants entry; the House decides (`03 §7.4`). There is no
"your friend is in!" — a nomination is a candidacy submitted, its outcome the House's.

The reward for nominating well is **internal status** (`01 §6`), never a discount or a
credit: the interface surfaces standing, never a coupon. Friction is preserved by design —
nominating is a considered, deliberate act, not a one-tap "share your invite link." The
friction *is* the product (`01 §6`); removing it removes what is sold.

---

## 9. What the interface must never do

Defaults are gravity. These are the ones the House refuses:

- **No engagement dark patterns** — infinite scroll, streaks, badges, notification bait,
  dwell-time optimization, autoplay.
- **No volume-referral mechanics** — share-links, invite-N-friends, referral codes
  (`01 §6`). Nomination is the only entry-by-member, and it is scarce and filtered.
- **No transactional signup** — entry is never "create account / start free trial" (§3).
- **No exposure of the membrane in Phase 0** — no member counts, no roster, no occupancy
  signal that reveals thinness. The door is opaque by design (`01 §5`).
- *(Owned by `04-DESIGN`, referenced not decided here: no pure white, no gradients, no
  decorative shadows.)*

---

## 10. Open questions — resolved

- **Shape of "how I think"** (`03 §9.1`): stays inside `apparatus` in v1. The reasoning is
  embedded Work by Work; no separate method surface is built yet.
- **Precedence gap** (`03 §11.1`): dormant at launch (§4); a uniform ~72h default stands,
  revisited at the first member-first release.
- **Phase 0 door — thin vitrine.** The public sees the `thesis` of the published Dispatches
  (taste is provable) and an apply door; membership stays opaque — no counts, no roster, no
  Dossiers. Taste is shown; the membrane is not. *(This reconciles `01 §1`'s shop-window
  with `01 §5`'s opaque door: the vitrine proves taste, the room stays closed.)*
- **FOUNDING designation — back only.** Tracked as `cohort = FOUNDING`, never surfaced in
  the front in v1 (§3). No internal caste at launch.
- **Threshold — slow, validated.** Entry is House-validated, never self-serve (§3); for the
  hand-picked cohort, review *is* the reality, and the deliberate pass reads as selection.
